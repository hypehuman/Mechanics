 Revised: Jul 11, 2019                  EM-L2                              3012
                               (Earth-Moon Lagrange 2)       

  #1) The Earth-Moon Lagrange-2 point (EM-L2) is an equilibirium location where
       the Moon's gravitational field partially counters that of the Earth.

  #2) This point is past the Moon on the Earth-Moon line, about 116.783% of 
       the EM distance (416300 to 475000 km from Earth center, averaging 
       ~449600 km), or 16.783% of the EM distance from the Moon, in the 
       direction away from the Earth (59800 to 68300 km from Moon center, 
       averaging ~64600 km).

  #4) EM-L2 is an unstable equilibrium point. This means an object at that 
       location will remain there unless disturbed, whereupon it will move 
       away.  In practice, there will always be some disturbance. 

 Time-span available: 1900-Jan-01 to 2151-Jan-01

TIME

  Barycentric Dynamical Time ("TDB" or T_eph) output was requested. This
continuous coordinate time is equivalent to the relativistic proper time
of a clock at rest in a reference frame co-moving with the solar system
barycenter but outside the system's gravity well. It is the independent
variable in the solar system relativistic equations of motion.

  TDB runs at a uniform rate of one SI second per second and is independent
of irregularities in Earth's rotation.
